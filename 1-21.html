Lesson Number: 46 Relating Multiplication and Division

Problem 1
Write and solve equations for problems 1 and 2.
Four hundred ninety-five oil drums were on the first train. Seven hundred sixty-two oil drums were on the first two trains combined. How many oil drums were on the second train?

Equation: \( 495 + x = 762 \) or \( 762 - 495 = x \)

To find the number of drums on the second train, we subtract the first train's amount from the total.

\( 762 - 495 = 267 \)

Answer: 267 oil drums

Problem 2
Workers on a Montana ranch baled 82 bales of hay on the first day. They baled 92 bales of hay on the second day and 78 bales of hay on the third day. How many bales of hay did the workers bale in all three days?

Equation: \( 82 + 92 + 78 = x \)

Sum the bales from all three days:

  • \( 82 + 92 = 174 \)
  • \( 174 + 78 = 252 \)

Answer: 252 bales of hay

Problem 3
The decimal number three and seventy-eight hundredths is how much more than two and twelve hundredths?

Convert the words to numbers:

  • Three and seventy-eight hundredths = \( 3.78 \)
  • Two and twelve hundredths = \( 2.12 \)

Subtract to find the difference:

\( 3.78 - 2.12 = 1.66 \)

Answer: 1.66 (one and sixty-six hundredths)

Problem 4
a. Round 786 to the nearest hundred.
b. Round 786 to the nearest ten.

a. Nearest Hundred:
Look at the tens digit (8). Since 8 is 5 or greater, round up.
Answer: 800

b. Nearest Ten:
Look at the ones digit (6). Since 6 is 5 or greater, round up the tens digit (8 becomes 9).
Answer: 790

Problem 5
Draw and shade rectangles to show the number \( 2 \frac{1}{3} \).

You need to draw 3 identical rectangles. Shade the first two completely (representing the whole number 2). Divide the third rectangle into 3 equal parts and shade 1 of them (representing \( \frac{1}{3} \)).

Problem 6
The first five odd numbers are 1, 3, 5, 7, and 9.
a. What is their sum?
b. What is the square root of their sum?

a. Sum:
\( 1 + 3 + 5 + 7 + 9 = 25 \)

b. Square Root:
The square root of 25 is the number that times itself equals 25.
\( \sqrt{25} = 5 \)

Problem 7
The clock shows a morning time. What time was it 12 hours before that time?

The clock shows 10:10.

Since it is a "morning time", it is 10:10 AM.

12 hours before any AM time is the same numerical time in PM of the previous day.

Answer: 10:10 PM

Problem 8
What type of angle is formed by the hands of the clock?

The minute hand is at 12. The hour hand is at 4.

This covers 4 "hour" sections of the clock. Each section is \( 30^\circ \) (360 / 12).
\( 4 \times 30^\circ = 120^\circ \).

An angle greater than \( 90^\circ \) and less than \( 180^\circ \) is Obtuse.

Problem 10
Kita took two dozen BIG steps. About how many meters did she walk?

1. "Two dozen" means \( 2 \times 12 = 24 \) steps.

2. A "BIG step" is commonly used to estimate 1 meter.

3. \( 24 \text{ steps} \times 1 \text{ meter/step} = 24 \text{ meters} \).

Answer: About 24 meters.

Problem 26
Draw a rectangle 3 in. long and 1 in. wide.
a. What is its perimeter?
b. What is its area?

a. Perimeter:
Perimeter = \( 2 \times (\text{length} + \text{width}) \)
\( 2 \times (3 + 1) = 2 \times 4 = 8 \text{ in.} \)

b. Area:
Area = \( \text{length} \times \text{width} \)
\( 3 \times 1 = 3 \text{ sq. in.} \)

Problem 27
Rosario noticed that the distance from the pole in the center of the tetherball circle to the painted circle was about six feet. What was the approximate radius of the tetherball circle?
A 12    B 4 yd    C 3 ft    D 2 yd

The "distance from the center pole to the painted circle" is the definition of the radius.

Radius = 6 feet.

Convert 6 feet to yards (since 3 feet = 1 yard):
\( 6 \text{ ft} \div 3 = 2 \text{ yards} \).

Answer: D (2 yd)

Problem 28
Tyrique, Dominic, and Tamsha checked their pockets for change.
  • Tyrique had two dimes and a penny.
  • Dominic had three nickels and two pennies.
  • Tamsha had a nickel, a dime, and a penny.
Using dollar signs and decimal points list the three amounts in order from least to greatest.

Calculate Totals:

  • Tyrique: 2 dimes ($0.20) + 1 penny ($0.01) = $0.21
  • Dominic: 3 nickels ($0.15) + 2 pennies ($0.02) = $0.17
  • Tamsha: 1 nickel ($0.05) + 1 dime ($0.10) + 1 penny ($0.01) = $0.16

Order (Least to Greatest):

$0.16, $0.17, $0.21

Problem 29
What is the twelfth term of the sequence below?
12, 24, 36, 48, 60, ...

This sequence consists of the multiples of 12.

1st term: \( 12 \times 1 = 12 \)
2nd term: \( 12 \times 2 = 24 \)

The 12th term will be: \( 12 \times 12 \)

Answer: 144

Problem 30
Write a rule that describes the relationship of the data in the table.
Number of Teachers12345
Number of Students714212835

Look at the relationship between the top row (Teachers) and the bottom row (Students).

  • \( 1 \times 7 = 7 \)
  • \( 2 \times 7 = 14 \)
  • \( 3 \times 7 = 21 \)

The number of students is always 7 times the number of teachers.

Rule: Multiply by 7 (or \( y = 7x \))
