Section 1: Measurement & Data

25. Which of these metric units would probably be used to describe the height of a tree?
  • A millimeters
  • B centimeters
  • C meters
  • D kilometers

Answer: C (meters)

Explanation: Trees are typically large objects, often ranging from 5 to 50 meters tall. Millimeters and centimeters are too small (used for bugs or paper), and kilometers are too large (used for driving distances).

26. Emily has a 2-liter bottle full of water and an empty half-gallon carton. She knows 1 liter is a little more than 1 quart. If she pours water from the bottle into the carton, what will happen?
  • A The bottle will be empty before the carton is full.
  • B The carton will be full before the bottle is empty.
  • C When the carton is full, the bottle will be empty.
  • D The carton will be empty, and the bottle will be full.

Answer: B

Explanation:
1 half-gallon = 2 quarts.
We know 1 liter > 1 quart.
Therefore, 2 liters > 2 quarts.
Since the bottle holds more than the carton, the carton will fill up and there will still be water left in the bottle.

27. Here is a list of selling prices for five houses. Arrange the prices in order from highest selling price to lowest selling price.

$179,500
$248,000
$219,000
$315,000
$232,000

Answer: $315,000, $248,000, $232,000, $219,000, $179,500

Explanation: Look at the hundred-thousands place first, then the ten-thousands place to compare values.

28. Which group of decimal numbers is arranged in order from least to greatest?
  • A 0.23, 0.21, 0.25
  • B 0.25, 0.23, 0.21
  • C 0.21, 0.23, 0.25
  • D 0.21, 0.25, 0.23

Answer: C

Explanation: Compare the digits in the hundredths place: 1 < 3 < 5. Thus, 0.21 < 0.23 < 0.25.

29. Three broken spaghetti pieces are \(1\frac{1}{2}\) inches, \(2\) inches, and \(2\frac{1}{4}\) inches long. If two of the three pieces are lined up end to end, what are all the possible combined lengths?

Answers: \(3\frac{1}{2}\) inches, \(3\frac{3}{4}\) inches, \(4\frac{1}{4}\) inches

Explanation: Calculate all pairs:
1. \(1\frac{1}{2} + 2 = 3\frac{1}{2}\)
2. \(1\frac{1}{2} + 2\frac{1}{4} = 1\frac{2}{4} + 2\frac{1}{4} = 3\frac{3}{4}\)
3. \(2 + 2\frac{1}{4} = 4\frac{1}{4}\)

30. Ra'Shawn ran a 100-meter dash in 16.5 seconds. Sabrina ran 0.4 seconds faster. What was Sabrina's time? Explain why your answer is reasonable.

Answer: 16.1 seconds

Explanation: "Faster" means a smaller amount of time. \[16.5 - 0.4 = 16.1\] Reasonableness: Since she was faster, her time should be less than Ra'Shawn's. 16.1 is slightly less than 16.5, so it makes sense.

Section 4: Word Problems & Geometry

1. 100 pennies are separated into two piles. One pile has 35 pennies. How many in the other?

65 pennies

\(100 - 35 = 65\)

2. Juan opened a 3.78 liter bottle of milk. He poured out 1.50 liters. How many liters left?

2.28 liters

\(3.78 - 1.50 = 2.28\)

3. San Francisco is 400 miles north of LA. Santa Barbara is 110 miles north of LA. Stephen drove from LA to Santa Barbara. How many miles does he still have to drive to reach San Francisco?

290 miles

Total distance (400) minus distance already traveled (110). \(400 - 110 = 290\).
