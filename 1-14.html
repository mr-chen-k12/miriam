#1
100 pennies are separated into two piles. In one pile there are 35 pennies. How many pennies are in the other pile?
(Write and solve an equation)
Answer: 65 pennies

Equation: \( 100 - 35 = n \) or \( 35 + n = 100 \)

Calculation: \( 100 - 35 = 65 \).

#2
Juan opened a 1-gallon bottle that held about 3.78 liters of milk. He poured about 1.50 liters into a pitcher. About how many liters were left in the bottle?
Answer: 2.28 liters

Subtract the amount poured from the total:

\( 3.78 - 1.50 = 2.28 \)

#3
San Francisco is 400 miles north of Los Angeles. Santa Barbara is 110 miles north of Los Angeles. Stephen drove from Los Angeles to Santa Barbara. How many miles does he still have to drive to reach San Francisco?
Answer: 290 miles

Subtract the distance already traveled from the total distance:

\( 400 - 110 = 290 \)

#4
Draw a rectangle that is 3 cm long and 3 cm wide.
a. What is the perimeter?
b. What is the area?
a. Perimeter: 12 cm

\( 3 + 3 + 3 + 3 = 12 \)

b. Area: 9 sq cm

\( 3 \times 3 = 9 \)

#25
Which of these metric units would probably be used to describe the height of a tree?
  • A. millimeters
  • B. centimeters
  • C. meters
  • D. kilometers
Answer: C (meters)

Trees are tall (usually 10-30 meters). Millimeters/centimeters are too small, kilometers are for long distances.

#26
Emily has a 2-liter bottle full of water and an empty half-gallon carton. She knows 1 liter is a little more than 1 quart. If she pours water from the bottle into the carton, what will happen?
  • A. The bottle will be empty before the carton is full.
  • B. The carton will be full before the bottle is empty.
  • C. When the carton is full, the bottle will be empty.
  • D. The carton will be empty, and the bottle will be full.
Answer: B

1 half-gallon = 2 quarts.
2 Liters > 2 Quarts.
So the Bottle holds more than the Carton. The Carton will fill up, and the Bottle will still have water.

#28
Which group of decimal numbers is arranged in order from least to greatest?
  • A. 0.23, 0.21, 0.25
  • B. 0.25, 0.23, 0.21
  • C. 0.21, 0.23, 0.25

#29
Three spaghetti pieces are \(1\frac{1}{2}\) inches, \(2\) inches, and \(2\frac{1}{4}\) inches. If two of them are lined up end to end, what are all the possible combined lengths?
Answers: \(3\frac{1}{2}\), \(3\frac{3}{4}\), \(4\frac{1}{4}\) inches

1. \(1.5 + 2 = 3.5\) (\(3\frac{1}{2}\))

2. \(1.5 + 2.25 = 3.75\) (\(3\frac{3}{4}\))

3. \(2 + 2.25 = 4.25\) (\(4\frac{1}{4}\))

#30
Ra'Shawn ran 100m in 16.5 seconds. Sabrina ran 0.4 seconds faster. What was Sabrina's time?
Answer: 16.1 seconds

Faster means less time. \(16.5 - 0.4 = 16.1\).
