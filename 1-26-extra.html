Radius vs. Diameter

Key Concept:
The Radius ($r$) is the distance from the center to the edge.
The Diameter ($d$) is the distance across the circle, passing through the center.
$$ d = 2 \times r $$
1. Look at the circle below. The red line represents the:
  • A. Diameter
  • B. Radius
  • C. Circumference
  • D. Chord
Correct Answer: B (Radius)
The line starts at the center and goes to the edge. That is the definition of a radius.
2. Look at the circle below. The blue line represents the:
  • A. Radius
  • B. Center
  • C. Diameter
  • D. Arc
Correct Answer: C (Diameter)
The line goes from one edge to the other and passes exactly through the center.
3. If the radius of a circle is 5 inches, what is the diameter?
  • A. 2.5 inches
  • B. 5 inches
  • C. 10 inches
  • D. 25 inches
Correct Answer: C (10 inches)
The diameter is twice the radius. $$ 5 \times 2 = 10 $$
4. A swimming pool has a diameter of 18 meters. What is the radius?
  • A. 9 meters
  • B. 36 meters
  • C. 18 meters
  • D. 6 meters
Correct Answer: A (9 meters)
The radius is half of the diameter. $$ 18 \div 2 = 9 $$
5. Which formula correctly shows the relationship between diameter ($d$) and radius ($r$)?
  • A. \( r = 2 \times d \)
  • B. \( d = r \div 2 \)
  • C. \( d = 2 \times r \)
  • D. \( d = r + 2 \)
Correct Answer: C
The diameter is equal to two times the radius.
6. Which line segment below is NOT a diameter?
  • A. The Green Line
  • B. The Purple Line
Correct Answer: A (The Green Line)
The Green line does not pass through the center. It is a chord, but not a diameter. The Purple line passes through the center, so it is a diameter.
7. A bike wheel has a spoke connecting the axle (center) to the rim (edge). This spoke represents the:
  • A. Circumference
  • B. Radius
  • C. Diameter
  • D. Area
Correct Answer: B (Radius)
Distance from center to edge is the radius.
8. If the radius is 3.5 cm, what is the diameter?
  • A. 6 cm
  • B. 7.5 cm
  • C. 3.5 cm
  • D. 7.0 cm
Correct Answer: D (7.0 cm)
$$ 3.5 + 3.5 = 7.0 $$
9. Circle A has a radius of 4 inches. Circle B has a diameter of 6 inches. Which circle is bigger?
  • A. Circle A
  • B. Circle B
  • C. They are the same size
Correct Answer: A (Circle A)
To compare, find the diameter of Circle A. $$ 4 \times 2 = 8 $$ inches.
Since 8 inches > 6 inches, Circle A is bigger.
10. Imagine a circle fits perfectly inside a square box. If the box is 10 inches wide, what is the radius of the circle?
  • A. 10 inches
  • B. 5 inches
  • C. 20 inches
  • D. 2.5 inches
Correct Answer: B (5 inches)
If the square is 10 inches wide, the diameter of the circle is 10 inches. The radius is half of that: 5 inches.
