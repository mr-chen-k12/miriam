Question 1
Brand A costs two dollars and forty-three cents. Brand B costs five dollars and seven cents. Brand B costs how much more than Brand A? Write an equation and solve this problem.
Answer: $2.64

Equation: \( 5.07 - 2.43 = x \)

To find the difference, we subtract the cost of Brand A from Brand B:

$$ \begin{array}{r} 5.07 \\ - 2.43 \\ \hline 2.64 \end{array} $$

Brand B costs $2.64 more than Brand A.

Question 2

Answer:

A fact family uses the same three numbers to create two multiplication and two division equations.

  1. \( 4 \times 5 = 20 \)
  2. \( 5 \times 4 = 20 \)
  3. \( 20 \div 4 = 5 \)
  4. \( 20 \div 5 = 4 \)
Question 3
What is the sum of the decimal numbers two and three tenths and eight and nine tenths?
Answer: 11.2

First, convert the words to numbers:

  • "Two and three tenths" = \( 2.3 \)
  • "Eight and nine tenths" = \( 8.9 \)

Now, add them together:

$$ \begin{array}{r} 2.3 \\ + 8.9 \\ \hline 11.2 \end{array} $$

(Note: \(3 + 9 = 12\), so you write down 2 and carry over the 1).

Question 4
Use digits 1, 5, 6, and 8 to write an even number greater than 8420. Each digit may be used only once.
Answer: 8516

To be greater than 8420, the first digit must be 8.

To be an even number, the last digit must be even (6 or 8). Since we already used 8 for the first digit, the last digit must be 6.

We have the digits 1 and 5 remaining for the middle spots.

Our structure is: \( 8 \_ \_ 6 \)

We need the number to be greater than 8420.
If we write 8156, it is less than 8420.
If we write 8516, it is greater than 8420.

Therefore, the number is 8516.

Question 5
A. Compare: \( 1 \frac{1}{2} \) and 1.75.
B. Use words to write the greater of the two numbers you compared in part a.
Answer: A) <, B) One and seventy-five hundredths

Part A: Convert fractions to decimals to compare.

\( 1 \frac{1}{2} = 1.50 \)

Comparing 1.50 and 1.75:

\( 1.50 < 1.75 \), so \( 1 \frac{1}{2} < 1.75 \).

Part B: The greater number is 1.75. In words, this is written as "One and seventy-five hundredths" (or simply "one point seven five").

Question 6
Carlos will use square floor tiles that measure one foot on each side to cover a hallway that is eight feet long and four feet wide. How many floor tiles will Carlos need?
Answer: 32 tiles

To find the number of tiles, we need to find the area of the hallway.

\( \text{Area} = \text{Length} \times \text{Width} \)

\( \text{Area} = 8 \text{ ft} \times 4 \text{ ft} = 32 \text{ sq ft} \)

Since each tile is 1 square foot (\(1 \times 1\)), he will need 32 tiles.

Question 8
a. Five dimes are what fraction of a dollar?
b. Write the value of five dimes using a dollar sign and decimal point.
Answer: a) \( \frac{1}{2} \) or \( \frac{50}{100} \), b) $0.50

Part A: A dime is 10 cents. 5 dimes = 50 cents.

A dollar is 100 cents.

Fraction = \( \frac{50}{100} \). This simplifies to \( \frac{1}{2} \).

Part B: 50 cents written as a dollar amount is $0.50.

Question 9
The length of segment PQ is 2 cm. The length of segment PR is 11 cm. How long is segment QR?
Answer: 9 cm

Based on the diagram, points P, Q, and R are on a line.

The total length PR is 11 cm.

The segment PQ is 2 cm.

To find QR, we subtract PQ from the total length PR:

\( QR = PR - PQ \)

\( QR = 11 - 2 = 9 \text{ cm} \)

Question 10
Which segment in this triangle appears to be perpendicular to segment AC?
Answer: Segment CB (or BC)

Perpendicular lines meet at a 90-degree angle (a right angle).

In the diagram, segment AC is vertical and segment CB is horizontal.

They meet at point C to form a right angle. Therefore, Segment CB is perpendicular to AC.

Question 11
Round 3296 to the nearest hundred.
Answer: 3300

1. Identify the hundreds digit: It is 2 (in 3296).

2. Look at the digit to the right (the tens place): It is 9.

3. Since 9 is 5 or greater, we round up the hundreds digit.

4. The 2 becomes a 3, and the following digits become zeros.

Result: 3300.

Question 13
Solve: \( 95 - (7 \times \sqrt{64}) \)
Answer: 39

Follow the Order of Operations (PEMDAS):

  1. Roots/Parentheses: Solve \( \sqrt{64} \). Since \( 8 \times 8 = 64 \), \( \sqrt{64} = 8 \).
  2. Multiplication: Now calculate \( 7 \times 8 = 56 \).
  3. Subtraction: Finally, solve \( 95 - 56 \).
$$ 95 - 56 = 39 $$

Question 26
Which of these does not show 24 divided by 4?
A. \( 24 \overline{)4} \)
B. \( \frac{24}{4} \)
C. \( 24 \div 4 \)
D. \( 4 \overline{)24} \)
Answer: A

Let's interpret each option:

  • B is \( 24 \div 4 \) (Fraction notation). Correct.
  • C is \( 24 \div 4 \) (Standard division sign). Correct.
  • D is long division notation where 4 is the divisor and 24 is the dividend (\( 24 \div 4 \)). Correct.
  • A is long division notation where 24 is the divisor and 4 is the dividend (\( 4 \div 24 \)). This is incorrect.

Question 28
Describe the order of operations in these expressions, and find the number each expression equals.
a. \( 12 \div (6 \div 2) \)
b. \( (12 \div 6) \div 2 \)
C. Does the Associative property apply to division?
Answer: a) 4, b) 1, c) No

Part A: Parentheses first. \( 6 \div 2 = 3 \). Then \( 12 \div 3 = 4 \).

Part B: Parentheses first. \( 12 \div 6 = 2 \). Then \( 2 \div 2 = 1 \).

Part C: Since the answers are different (4 vs 1), the grouping matters. Therefore, the Associative property does not apply to division.

Question 29
In the year 2003, each visitor to the country of Mexico spent an average of $540. Each visitor to the country of Canada spent an average of $557. How many more dollars did each visitor to Canada spend in 2003?
Answer: $17

Subtract the average spending in Mexico from the average spending in Canada.

\( 557 - 540 = 17 \)

Visitors spent $17 more in Canada.

Question 30
One of the largest hammerhead sharks ever caught weighed 991 pounds. One of the largest porbeagle sharks ever caught weighed 507 pounds. Round to the nearest hundred pounds to estimate the weight difference of those two sharks.
Answer: 500 pounds

1. Round the weights to the nearest hundred:

  • 991 rounds to 1000.
  • 507 rounds to 500.

2. Calculate the difference of the estimated weights:

\( 1000 - 500 = 500 \)

The estimated difference is 500 pounds.
