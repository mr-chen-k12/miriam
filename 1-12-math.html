Homework Solutions

Set 1 (Questions 1-8)

1. The large square represents 1. Write the shaded part of the square:
a. as a fraction
b. as a decimal number
c. using words

a. \(\frac{42}{100}\) (or \(\frac{21}{50}\))

b. \(0.42\)

c. Forty-two hundredths

2. Takeshi had a dime, a quarter, and a penny. Write this amount using a dollar sign and a decimal point.
\(10 + 25 + 1 = 36\) cents.
Answer: \(\$0.36\)
3. Donna opened a 1-gallon container of milk and poured 1 quart of milk into a pitcher. How many quarts of milk were left in the 1-gallon container?
1 gallon = 4 quarts.
\(4 - 1 = 3\).
Answer: 3 quarts
4. Describe the rule for this sequence and find the next three numbers:
..., 4200, 4300, 4400, ___, ___, ___ ...
Rule: Count up by 100s.
Next numbers: 4500, 4600, 4700
5. Use digits and a comparison symbol to show that the decimal number five tenths equals the fraction one half.
Answer: \(0.5 = \frac{1}{2}\)
6. Ananado fell asleep last night at the time shown on the clock (10:10). His alarm clock was set to ring eight hours later. What time was Ananado's alarm clock set to ring?
The clock shows 10:10.
\(10:10 + 8 \text{ hours} = 18:10\).
In 12-hour time, 18:10 is 6:10.
7. Find the missing factor: \(5w = 45\)
\(w = 45 \div 5\)
Answer: \(w = 9\)
8. The following was marked on the label of a juice container: 2 qt (1.89 L). Use words to write 1.89 L.
Answer: One and eighty-nine hundredths liters.

Set 2 (Questions 9-16)

9. What mixed number is illustrated by these shaded triangles?
One whole triangle is shaded, and 3 out of 4 parts of the second triangle are shaded.
Answer: \(1 \frac{3}{4}\)
10. Which letter below has no right angles?
F   E   Z   L
Answer: Z
11. Rewrite this addition problem as a multiplication problem:
\(\$1.25 + \$1.25 + \$1.25 + \$1.25\)
Answer: \(4 \times \$1.25\)
12. How long is the line segment to the nearest quarter inch?
The line goes past 1 inch and ends at the third tick mark (3/4).
Answer: \(1 \frac{3}{4}\) inches
13. A meter equals how many centimeters?
Answer: 100
14. a. Five dimes are what fraction of a dollar?
b. Write the value of five dimes using a dollar sign and a decimal point.

a. \(\frac{50}{100}\) or \(\frac{1}{2}\)

b. \(\$0.50\)

15. Compare:
a. \(0.5\) ___ \(0.50\)
b. \(\frac{1}{2}\) ___ \(\frac{1}{4}\)

a. \(0.5 = 0.50\)

b. \(\frac{1}{2} > \frac{1}{4}\)

16. Calculate:
a. \(3 \times 8\)    b. \(3 \times 7\)    c. \(3 \times 6\)    d. \(3 \times 12\)

a. 24

b. 21

c. 18

d. 36

Set 4 (Questions 25-30)

25. Use digits to write one million, fifty thousand.
Answer: 1,050,000
26. If the area of a square is 36 square inches, then how long is each side of the square?
\(s \times s = 36 \implies s = 6\)
Answer: A (6 in.)
27. The distance from Riley's house to school is 1.4 miles. Write 1.4 with words.
Answer: One and four tenths
28. Nieve quickly started and stopped a stopwatch four times. Write these times in order from fastest to slowest:
0.27 second, 0.21 second, 0.24 second, 0.20 second.
Fastest (smallest number) to slowest:
Answer: 0.20, 0.21, 0.24, 0.27
29. The Washington Monument is 153 feet taller than the City Center building in Nashville, Tennessee, which is 402 feet tall. How tall is the Washington Monument?
\(402 + 153 = 555\)
Answer: 555 feet
30. The Panther waterfall in Alberta is 600 feet tall. The Fall Creek waterfall in Tennessee is 256 feet tall. How many feet taller is the Panther waterfall? Explain how you found your answer.
Subtract the height of Fall Creek from Panther.
\(600 - 256 = 344\)
Answer: 344 feet
