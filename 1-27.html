Question 1
Write and solve equations for problems 1 and 2.
There were 8 boys in each row. There were 4 rows. How many boys were in all 4 rows?
Strategy: Equal groups usually implies multiplication.
We have 4 rows (groups) with 8 boys in each row.
Equation: \( 8 \times 4 = b \)
Answer: 32 boys
Question 2
There were 7 girls in each row. There were 9 rows. How many girls were in all 9 rows?
Strategy: Multiplication (groups of).
We have 9 rows with 7 girls in each row.
Equation: \( 7 \times 9 = g \)
Answer: 63 girls
Question 3

\( \begin{array}{r} 375 \\ - \phantom{0}75 \\ \hline 300 \end{array} \)

Question 7
Tika is a college student. She began her homework last night at the time shown on the clock. She finished two and one half hours later. What time did Tika finish her homework?
1. Read the clock: The hour hand is just past 7. The minute hand is on the 2. The time is 7:10.
2. Add Duration: "Two and one half hours" = 2 hours and 30 minutes.
\( 7:10 + 2 \text{ hours} = 9:10 \)
\( 9:10 + 30 \text{ minutes} = 9:40 \)
Answer: 9:40

Question 14
The length of segment RT is 9 cm. The length of segment ST is 5 cm. What is the length of segment RS?
(Diagram shows points R, S, T on a line)
Segment Addition Postulate: \( RS + ST = RT \)
Substitute known values: \( RS + 5 = 9 \)
Subtract 5 from both sides: \( RS = 9 - 5 \)
Answer: 4 cm

Question 26
The radius of a circle is 3 ft. Which of the following is not the diameter of the circle?
A. 36 in.
B. 6 ft
C. 2 yd
D. 72 in.
Fact: Diameter = 2 × Radius.
Diameter = \( 2 \times 3 \text{ ft} = 6 \text{ ft} \).
Let's check the options:
A. 36 in = 3 ft (This is the radius, not the diameter).
B. 6 ft (This IS the diameter).
C. 2 yd = 6 ft (This IS the diameter).
D. 72 in = 6 ft (This IS the diameter).
Answer: A

Question 29
One human hand has 27 bones. One human foot has 26 bones. About how many bones are in two hands plus two feet? Explain why your estimate is reasonable.
Estimation Strategy: Round to the nearest ten.
27 rounds up to 30.
26 rounds up to 30.
Two hands + Two feet = \( 30 + 30 + 30 + 30 \)
Calculation: \( 4 \times 30 = 120 \)
Exact math for checking: \( 27+27+26+26 = 106 \). 120 is a reasonable high-end estimate, or you could estimate 25+25+25+25 = 100 for a closer low-end estimate. Given standard rounding rules, 120 is the standard answer.
Estimate: About 120 bones (or 110/100 depending on rounding method).
Question 30
The land area of Booker T. Washington National Monument in Virginia is 239 acres. The land area of Cabrillo National Monument is 160 acres. What is a reasonable estimate of the total acreage of these two national monuments? Explain why your estimate is reasonable.
Estimation Strategy: Round to the nearest ten or compatible numbers.
239 rounds to 240.
160 stays 160.
Calculation: \( 240 + 160 = 400 \)
Answer: 400 acres. This is reasonable because we rounded 239 up by 1, making the mental math 24+16=40 extremely simple.
