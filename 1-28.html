Problem 1
Write and solve equations for problems 1-3.
Each of the 3 boats carried 12 people. In all, how many people were in 3 boats?

To find the total number of people, multiply the number of boats by the people per boat.

Equation: \( 3 \times 12 = p \)

Calculation: \( 3 \times 12 = 36 \)

Answer: 36 people

Problem 5
Justin ran the perimeter of the block. How far did Justin run? The measurements of the block are shown on the figure below (100 yd by 50 yd).

\( P = 100 + 50 + 100 + 50 \)

\( P = 150 + 150 = 300 \)

Problem 8
Round 783 and 217 to the nearest hundred. Then subtract the smaller rounded number from the larger rounded number.

Step 1: Rounding

  • 783 rounded to the nearest hundred is 800 (since 83 > 50).
  • 217 rounded to the nearest hundred is 200 (since 17 < 50).

Step 2: Subtraction

\( 800 - 200 = 600 \)

Answer: 600

Problem 25
The length of segment AB is 5cm. The length of segment BC is 4cm. What is the length of segment AC?

Segment AC is the sum of segment AB and segment BC.

\( AC = AB + BC \)

\( AC = 5 + 4 \)

Answer: 9 cm

Problem 28
Which of the following is more than one second but less than two seconds?
A. 0.15 sec
B. 1.5 sec
C. 2.1 sec
D. 2.15 sec

We are looking for a number \( x \) where \( 1 < x < 2 \).

  • 0.15 is less than 1.

Problem 30
The thermometers show the average daily minimum and maximum temperatures in New York City's Central Park during July. What is the difference in degrees between the two temperatures?

Reading the thermometers:

Minimum (Left): The level is 1 tick mark above 70. Assuming 2-degree increments, that is 72°.

Maximum (Right): The level is 2 tick marks above 80. That is 84°.

Difference:

\( 84 - 72 = 12 \)

Answer: 12 degrees
